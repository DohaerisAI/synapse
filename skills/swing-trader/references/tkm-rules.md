# TKM Position Management Rules

By AD–Ritesh. These are non-negotiable rules for every swing trade.

## Capital Allocation

- Keep swing trading capital separate from long-term and intraday funds
- Ideally use separate demat accounts for each purpose (also simplifies tax filing)
- Minimum swing capital: ₹1 lakh. Below this, returns are too small to justify the effort.

## Position Sizing

- Divide total swing capital into **6–8 equal parts**
- Each trade gets exactly one part — never double down, never oversize
- Maximum open trades at any time: **6–8**
- Equal rupee allocation per trade, not equal share quantity

### Risk Per Trade

- Maximum risk: **1–2% of total swing capital** per trade
- If the SL distance forces more than 2% risk at full allocation, **reduce quantity**

**Formula:**
```
max_quantity = (per_trade_capital × max_risk_pct) / SL_distance_per_share
```

**Example:**
- Total capital: ₹1,00,000
- Parts: 7 → per trade: ~₹14,286
- Max risk at 1.5%: ₹1,500
- SL distance: ₹20/share
- Max qty: 1500 / 20 = 75 shares
- Actual investment: 75 × entry_price

If entry is ₹1,275 → investment = ₹95,625 (within the per-trade allocation)
If entry is ₹250 → investment = ₹18,750 (exceeds ₹14,286 allocation)
In the second case, cap at allocation: ₹14,286 / ₹250 = 57 shares

**Rule: take the LOWER of risk-based qty and allocation-based qty.**

## Risk-Reward

- Minimum R:R = **1:2**. No exceptions.
- Ideal R:R = 1:2.5 to 1:3
- If the chart doesn't offer 1:2, **skip the trade** no matter how good the pattern looks
- Calculate R:R before entering, not after

**Why 1:2 minimum works:**
Even at 50% win rate with 1:2 R:R:
- 10 trades: 5 wins × 2R + 5 losses × 1R = 10R - 5R = +5R net profit
- You can lose more trades than you win and still be profitable

## Weekly Loss Cap

- If 3 consecutive SLs are hit in a week: **stop trading for the rest of the week**
- Reassess on Monday. Check if market regime has changed.
- This prevents emotional overtrading after losses

## Correlation Check

Before entering a new trade, check your open positions:
- Don't hold 3–4 stocks from the same sector (that's one sector bet, not 4 trades)
- Maximum 2 stocks from the same sector at any time
- Diversify across: banking, IT, pharma, auto, FMCG, metal, energy, realty

## Capital Below Minimum

If available swing capital is below ₹1 lakh:
- Do not swing trade
- Advise the user to build capital through SIP, savings, or other means
- Swing trading with small capital leads to frustration and poor risk management
